# Lutin Trademark Policy

The Lutin source code is licensed under [GPL-3.0](LICENSE). You are free to
fork it, modify it, and redistribute it under the same license.

The **Lutin name, the Lutin logo, and the official signed binaries** are
**not** covered by that license. They are unregistered trademarks of Nicolas
("Halloweedev"), the project maintainer. This document describes how they may
and may not be used.

The goal of this policy is simple: when a user downloads something called
**Lutin** or **Lutin.app**, they should be confident it is the official build
produced by this project — signed, notarized, and unmodified.

## You **may**, without asking permission

- Use the name "Lutin" in **descriptive, factual references** — for example:
  - "Built with Lutin"
  - "A tutorial on packaging DMGs with Lutin"
  - "Lutin is a macOS DMG editor"
- Link to the official repository (https://github.com/Halloweedev/lutin) and
  the official website.
- Reproduce the Lutin logo at reasonable sizes in articles, blog posts,
  videos, and presentations that describe or review the project, provided the
  logo is not modified and is clearly used to refer to *this* project.

## You **may not**, without written permission

- Distribute a modified build of Lutin under the name "Lutin", "Lutin.app",
  "Lutin CLI", or any name confusingly similar (e.g. "Lutin Pro", "Lutin+",
  "LutinX", "MyLutin").
- Use the Lutin name or logo in a way that suggests your product, service,
  fork, or company is endorsed by, affiliated with, or the official source
  of Lutin when it is not.
- Use the Lutin name or logo as the primary brand for a paid product or
  service.
- Modify the Lutin logo and present the modified version as the Lutin logo.
- Register a domain name, social media handle, package name (Homebrew tap,
  npm, etc.), or trademark that incorporates "Lutin" in a way that could
  mislead users into thinking it is the official project.

## Forks

You are welcome and encouraged to fork Lutin — that's what GPL-3.0 is for.
If you distribute a fork as a binary or a packaged release, please:

1. **Rename the binary, the app bundle, and any user-facing branding** so
   that users cannot mistake your fork for the official Lutin.
2. Keep the GPL-3.0 license intact and publish your source as the GPL
   requires.
3. You may state, factually, that your project is "a fork of Lutin" or
   "based on Lutin", and link back to this repository.

## Questions, or asking for permission

If you'd like to use the Lutin name or logo in a way this policy does not
explicitly allow — for a conference talk, a course, a commercial partnership,
a fork that you'd like to call something Lutin-adjacent — please open a
[GitHub Discussion](https://github.com/Halloweedev/lutin/discussions) or
reach out to the maintainer. We're friendly and the answer is usually yes
with small adjustments.
